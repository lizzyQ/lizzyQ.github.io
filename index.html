Chinese Go

Language tutoring site Go ↣

Tags
Hard-coding HTML/CSS jQuery php/mySQL Non Profit Education Responsive PSD to HTML

Requirements

  • Custom Back-end
  • Mobile Accessibility
  • Index Page Design in PSD
  • UX / UI

Phase1 - Business studying and UI Inspiration
The idea behind Chinese Go is providing an open platform for Chinese language learners who can easily access their free studying source and communicate with each other.
Among all the materials online which are so much range in quality, The way that Chinese go stands out from the crowd is by providing well-organized contents as well as having playful elements that encouraging the learner to explore more.
The UI of the landing page should be clean and aesthetic. I started by searching through the Chinese ink painting and used the pastel colors – soft teals and light sky blue with ash gray – to give the impression of a traditional Chinese paint. The subpages consist with the color choice, whereas arranged more clean and structured.
The landing page acts as navigation which is a new and playful experience.

Phase2 - Concepts to PSD
To achieve the ink painting look alike designing, I draw the whole picture in Photoshop, Within mind the position of the navigation. But the whole paint idea is very limited by the size of the screen. For a smaller screen, a different solution was required. An extra paint with the same color tone was designed for the responsive demand.
For the matter of multiple language control, the category card was also designed in PhotoShop. The beautiful paint really enticing visitors to explore the site further.

Phase3 - PSD to HTML/CSS
Attracting the user’s interest is a big concern for the landing page. Merging menus into the images force the user to move their mouse around and find the way to start.
The landing page was powered by css3, slightly used javascript for cross-browser compatibility. The small screen resolution was not finished at the time. After finished the initial coding for desktop, I started to dig more in PHP and MySQL.

Phase4 - Backend Development
This project is sone of my undergoing work. It’s the first site that I am doing the backend developing part by myself. Learning and working on a project at the same time is so much fun. I am able to try out different functions and debugging myself. I hope this site be finished anytime soon and really become a useful tool for Chinese learners.

Fine Line

WordPress Theme developing with Bootstrap Go ↣

Tags
Bootstarp WordPress Theme B2B Responsive

Requirements

  • Custom WordPress
  • Quality Assurance
  • Social Media Referral
  • Prototyping

Phase1 - Behind scene story
Kim is a photographer and friend of mine. She previously using her Instagram as a showcase of her unique work. Wishing to show her creations in a more organized way, she needed a website to reflect the high quality of her sensual artwork. A potential customer contacted her asked for her portfolio, that’s when Kim reached me and requested a “For Emergency Usage” website.
I used bootstrap to speed up, and showed her an expediency in 2 days. Yet it did not convey the professional nature of her photograph. Based on the rough foundation, I decided to push it further to develop a complete, trendy website for her.
The style of her photo works have a hint of vintage feeling, very like an old photo from 80’s Japanese post. It’s like a line of photo that wandering between old time and modern time. I suggest to keep the style of the website be minimal, to evoke a fresh sense of welcoming and acceptance.

Phase2 - Prototyping and Markup
We wanted to make the site clean, clear, simple and memorable. we picked up a subtle palette of vivid gray color from one of her photo products. A full screen automatic slide with text overlay sits on top of the contemporary navigation bar. The fixed left side area is clean and informative. The typography feels friendly. The visitors will learn the necessary message in a glance effortlessly.
Making the content highly visual was a key objective. With the flex image grid, the artworks in different size move seamlessly. The experience is intuitive and harmonious.

Phase3 - WordPress Integration
Kim has over hundred art piece to be uploaded. To be able to independently make any updates or amendments to her site, A nice content management system will be a big benefit for both of us. That’s where WordPress came to handy. I am proficient in the designing and markup process, whereas for WordPress which requires solid knowledge of PHP, will take me long. Yes, this is another undergoing project.

Web Designer's Potofolio Site

Tags

Requirements

Overview
The old way I do web designing is having PSD file first then slice tons of images. This project helped me to get rid of the habit that relying on PSD file and start the web designing directly from markup language Which saves a lot of time and the design is more flexible. The profile site is a mock-up of an award winning website. During the process, I learned about responsive designing. Normally, A website will need to be redeveloped every 5 years to take advantage of the latest enhancements and styling options. This one is 4 years old, I would say it still looks quite neat.

Design analyst
Utilizing a full-screen automatic slideshow as background gives the site a vibrant feeling. Bright orange, white with translucent black were the only color used for designing which brings an elegant atmosphere. The thumbnail images with text overlay provides more information. The design has good consistency, completed professionally.

One of the most satisfying parts of web developing is seeing the transformation of a web page to current development standards. So I took this one off from the host for future upgrading, you may e-mail me for the code.

Involving and Discussing The Political Activity(2012)

Web App

Tags
Hard-coding HTML/CSS B2C Responsive PSD to HTML

Overview
Involving and Discussing The Political Activity was the initial and paramount project I worked on when I was working as the web designer in a web agency in Korea.
The owner wanted to use the website to edit and send out text messages as well as images and videos. The site should behave like a message manager system and a web application, it’s function oriented also mobile oriented. I was giving this task with the landing page PSD file, my duty was transfering images to markup languages. You may think the landing page is just a picture, but actually every element inside of the phone works. The visitors may choose template, editing text, and add images or videos then send it out as a text messages.
S.Korea is one of the few countries that has majority of Internet Explorer users. The hardest part of this task is cross browser compatability. Make it look exactly same for different browser was tricky. I left the job after I done, so I don’t know the published domain of this site.
